Methods

Effective communication is key for team and project success. The list below aggregates common methods and deliverables, which I reshape for team and project requirements.

Agile UX Design Methods

Project Goals

A map that defines key project goals in the early stages of a project to supports the team narrow down and create consensus around what the product will be.

Research

A way to explore and share industry standards, including comprehensive analysis of competitor products, and identify opportunities to innovate in a given area.

Workshop Moodboards

A collaborative collection of images and references to inspire creative thinking within a project brief, it may also evolve into a projects visual style guide.

User Trials

Provide market information by allowing consumers to test a new product concept, which can influence design and sales strategies.

Personas

Personas make it easier for designers to create empathy with consumers throughout the design process.

Storyboards

Illustrate product functionalities into real-life situations, helping designers create empathy with the consumer while having a first look at the product scope.

Brainstorming

Allows the team to explore and visualize a broad range of design solutions before deciding which one to stick with.

Sketches

A quick way to visualize and validate product concepts and design approaches both with team members and users.
