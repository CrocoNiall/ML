User Experiance (UX)
Methods

Effective communication is key for team and project success. I reshape these methods on the go to suit the audience’s needs and requirements.

Agile UX Methods

Design Strategy

Project Goals

Together with the company directors and clients, I plan the scope of a product application, the features and integrations. I communicate design strategies using mind maps, competitive analysis, consumer journey maps, user stories and personas.

Research

I focus my research methods to understand user behaviors, needs and motivation through observation techniques, task analysis, published research papers and other feedback methodologies. A product application is only as good as the research behind it.

Personas

I represent the key audience segments using personas to express major user expectations and needs for reference when making design decisions.

Workflow Diagrams

I create diagrams that explores the multiple, sometimes invisible, steps taken by consumers as they engage with the service. These diagrams help to create design solutions appropriate for each step.

Concepting Methods

Brainstorming

I lead brainstorming sessions with clients, company partners and colleagues to kick of new projects. They usually involve a presentation outlining a project and workshop activities to explore and visualize a broad range of design solutions.

Workshop Moodboards

I create abstract collages in workshops to inspire and focus creative thinking at the beginning of a project. I also collaborate images and references that will eventually evolve into a products style guide, which I show to clients and colleagues to communicate style direction.

Storyboards

I use High-level storyboard illustrations as a great tool for testing product viability and user experience strategy with users and clients early in the UX process.

User Research and Validation Methods

User Trials

I lead and facilitate user trials to evaluate product concepts by testing them on users. I am currently in the process of publishing my first research paper, which I collaborated with Teesside University. My research so far has established revolutionary lighting design concepts with media and the effects coloured light has on user’s moods. I have then designed new tools to author these experiences and presented these ideas to Innovate UK.

Qualitative research

I enter local events where I can build and demonstrate new lighting installation concepts and user experiences to the public and gather user feedback. Feedback usually is collected in the form of qualitative and quantitative surveys.

User Interface Design

Sketches

I visualize new interfaces, products and light experiences using pen and paper as a quick way to communicate and develop product concepts and design approaches. I carry a notebook wherever I go as I can be inspired with new product solutions and creative thoughts anyplace, anytime.

Wireframes

I draw a huge amount of wireframes to improve the user experience and constantly test them by developing prototypes and obtaining user feedback.

Prototypes

I create and assemble paper prototypes and Axure prototypes as a great way to refine and validate a product before fully developing it.
